Laying Down the Sword

· · ·

I acknowledge that I have been armed — with criticism, with comparison, with the need to be right — and that these weapons have wounded the men in my life and, in the end, myself.

Today, of my own free will, I lay down my sword.

I vow to look for the good reasons behind what I do not yet understand. I vow to receive before I instruct. I vow to extend the benefit of the doubt when my old habits would draw a blade instead.

I make this commitment not because it is easy, but because I want something more beautiful than being right. I want to be in partnership. I want to be free.

— The Queen's Code
